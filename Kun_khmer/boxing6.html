Cellcard backs National Kun Khmer Championship


Cellcard, Cambodia’s leading telecommunications company, has committed to supporting the National Kun Khmer Championship in the lead up to the 2021 Southeast Asian Games in Vietnam.

Cellcard made the announcement as the championship commences at the Old Army Stadium in Phnom Penh. The annual event provides a platform for athletes to showcase their skills and abilities, while also serving as a venue for the selection of national team players.

The championship is being organised by the Kun Khmer Federation (KKF) under the guidance of the Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport and the National Olympic Committee Cambodia. It was supposed to be held earlier, but like many other sports activities in Cambodia, it was delayed by the coronavirus pandemic.

The National Kun Khmer Championship features the top Cambodian fighters in various weight divisions.

General Tem Moeun, president of the KKF, opened the ceremony with his welcome remarks, followed by Cellcard Chief Executive Officer Ian Watson.

Watson said the association was firmly aligned to Cellcard’s position as the only 100 percent Cambodian-owned mobile communications company in the Kingdom.

“As Cellcard stands for traditional Khmer values and heritage it’s a great fit to support Kun Khmer which is very much a part of Cambodia’s rich history and sporting pride,” he stressed.

“Cellcard and Royal Group are truly proud to support our nation’s athletes as they put their best foot forward in flying the Cambodian flag at the games,” Watson added.

The National Kun Khmer Championship will conclude on Jan 29.

This new development follows Cellcard’s announcement last year of its two-year partnership with Singapore-based ONE Championship, one of the largest sports entertainment properties in the world. The groundbreaking tie-up with ONE Championship makes it easier and more affordable for Cambodians to gain access to ONE’s digital content whenever and wherever they are.
